What This Is

Some stories deserve
to be heard.

You don't need a studio. You don't need a record deal. You don't need to have lived a perfect life — or even a beautiful one.

You just need your truth. And a way to shape it into something that holds it.

The Forever Still Method walks you through exactly that — step by step, question by question — until the thing you've been carrying becomes a song you can finally feel.

The Method

Eight steps. One real song.

01

Your Foundation

Your Story Foundation

Who You Are (The Real You)

Before we write your song, we need to understand you. You don't need perfect answers. Just honest ones.

  • Where did you grow up?
  • What kind of person were you as a kid?
  • What shaped you the most growing up?
Tip: Write like you're finally being understood.
02

The Hard Parts

The Moments That Changed You

Every powerful song has truth in it. This is where yours begins.

You can go as deep as you're ready for. There's no pressure here.

  • What's something you've been through that changed you?
  • What is a moment you still think about often?
  • What felt like too much to carry?
Optional deeper prompt: What did you need at that moment… that no one gave you?
03

Your Strength

What You Survived (And Became)

There is beauty in what you survived. You're still here for a reason.

  • What did you make it through?
  • When did you realize you were stronger than you believed?
  • What keeps you going even when life gets heavy?

This is where your strength becomes your voice.

04

Relationships

The People Who Shaped Your Story

Some people leave a mark that never fades.

  • Who has had the biggest impact on your life?
  • What did they teach you — intentionally or not?
  • Is there something you wish they understood about you?
Creative prompt: If they heard this song… what would you want them to finally understand?
05

Your Truth

What People Don't See

Some truths are hard to say out loud. That doesn't make them any less real.

Be honest here. This is just for you.

  • What do people get wrong about you?
  • What do you hide or keep quiet about?
  • What would you say if you knew you wouldn't be judged?

This is where your truth becomes your voice.

06

Your Message

What This Song Is Really About

Every song says something. What does yours say?

If someone heard your story… what would you want them to take with them?

  • If your life had a message, what would it be?
  • What do you want someone going through this to hear?
  • What truth do you wish someone had told you sooner?
Finish this sentence:
"Even when ________, you can still ________."
This is the heart of your song.
07

Song Direction

Let's Build Your Song

You've found your story. Now let's decide how it should feel.

Think about the kind of song that would carry your message.

  • What genre fits your story? (pop, cinematic, acoustic, R&B…)
  • What should this song feel like? (sad, hopeful, powerful, reflective)
  • What pace matches your emotion? (slow / mid / upbeat)
  • What kind of voice do you hear? (female, male, soft, strong)
  • What artists or songs does it remind you of?

This is the sound of your story.

08

Your Moment

You Did Something Powerful

You just turned your life into art. That's something most people never do.

Don't overthink it. Don't judge it. Just feel it.

Read your song back. Let it sit with you. You've done something real — and that matters more than you know.
