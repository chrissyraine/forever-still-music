Her Story

I didn't stumble into this — I built it.

Since February, I've reached over 24,000 plays on SoundCloud, with 185 likes and 39 comments from real listeners. That's not luck — that's connection.

But none of this came easy.

I'm a U.S. Army veteran who stepped away from the world for nearly a decade to rebuild my life and protect my children after surviving serious trauma. I chose safety. I chose survival. I built a quiet, stable life from nothing.

This year, I made the decision to step back into the world — publicly, creatively, and without hiding.

That wasn't easy. It went against everything I had trained myself to do to stay safe. But I did it anyway — because I'm ready.

I've been an artist my entire life — singing, performing, creating, even winning awards for photography long before AI existed. AI didn't make me an artist — it gave me a way to keep creating while raising two young boys on my own.

What I'm building now blends emotional storytelling with modern tools, rooted in resilience, motherhood, and personal transformation.

I've faced setbacks — even recently being placed in mentorship situations that didn't align and forced me to defend the validity of my work. But every time something tries to slow me down, it sharpens me.

I learn. I adapt. I get stronger.

Because I have two boys watching me. Because I refuse to shrink. Because I know what I'm building is real.

And I'm not stopping.

"AI is the vessel.
The story is mine."

— Chrissy Schroer, Forever Still Music
